Vision Arm

Cycloidal Robotic Arm - Vision Capabilities

  • 6 Degrees of Freedom
  • Open CV & AprilTags Detection
  • Unity Arm and Detection Visualizer

Mechanical & Hardware Design

  • 60 cm Reach, 2 Kg Payload
  • 4 Cycloidal Drives - 2 Planetary Gearboxes
  • Completely 3D Printed
  • Driven by 6 Stepper Motors
  • Limit Switch Homing
  • Joints 5 & 6 are driven by 2 motors with a differential
  • Designed in Fusion 360

Cycloidal Drives

  • The majority of the joints used Cycloidal Drives
  • Apart from the Bearing and Steel Rods, the gearboxes were entirely 3D printed
  • The Image to the left is all of the components of Joint 2

Electronics & Control

  • 4 Nema 17s and 2 Nema 23 Stepper Motors
  • Controlled by an Arduino Mega with 2 CNC Shields
  • Each Stepper Motor has a closed loop driver board with a Magnetic Encoder
  • The whole system runs on a 12V power supply
  • The camera plugs directly into an external computer

Vision Pipeline

  1. The arm orients the camera over the workspace
  2. A picture is taken and sent to an external computer's WSL
  3. In the WSL the AprilTag and cubes are detected
  4. The AprilTag's known dimensions are used to convert pixel distances to millimeters
  5. Finally, the cube positions are sent back to Windows from the WSL for visualization

Inverse Kinematics

  • Gradient Descent is the method I used as it was the easiest at the time
  • Joint angles are computed in Unity and sent to the Arduino
  • The arm moves in every direction until it is as close as it can get to the desired postion

Visualizer

  • This Visualizer is run in Unity
  • It takes the Positions of the Cubes, displays them, and allows the user to move the arm accordingly
  • When commanded, the program sends the Joint Angles to the Arduino to move the arm
