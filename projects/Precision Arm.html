Precision Robotic Arm

Harmonic Drive Robotic Arm - Highly Precise

  • 6 Degrees of Freedom
  • Dual On-Axis Encoders
  • End-effector repeatability ≈ ±0.75 mm
  • 1 m reach, 5 kg Payload

Mechanical & Hardware Design

  • The body is 3D printed apart from the carbon fiber tubes spanning the large distances
  • Joints 1-3 are driven by large 90Kv Brushless Motors
  • Joints 4-6 are driven by smaller 360Kv Brushless Motors
  • No homing is needed! Each Joint has an Absolute Encoder
  • Designed in Fusion 360

Harmonic Drive Gearboxes

  • This Arm uses special 3D printed harmonic drives which are a whole project in themselves
  • With some really good engineering, they have an 16 bit on-axis encoder in the center of the FlexSpline
  • The image on the left is a cross-section of the smaller Joints
  • Click here to see more

Electronics & Control

  • All 6 motors are controlled by Moteus R4s
  • The motor controllers are daisy chained and communicate with CAN-FD
  • The 16 bit Joint Encoders connect directly to the Moteus R4s allowing the entire Arm to be controlled by one USB port!
  • The Arm is powered by a 24V PSU with a Buck Converter for the 5V Gripper
  • The Arm plugs into a Pi5 connected to a screen and Wi-Fi

ROS 2

  • The Pi 5 Subscribes to the Joint Angles via ROS 2
  • The Joint Angles are generated by Moveit 2, a ROS 2 Inverse Kinematics Package
  • The Visualizer is commonly run on an external computer on the same network
  • Although the Moteus controllers support ROS, I prefered to make my own script that subscribes to the Angles and manually drives the motors
  • Everything ROS related is written in Python

Inverse Kinematics

  • Inverse Kinematics are calculated by the ROS 2 Package MoveIt
  • It uses a URDF that was generated from Fusion 360
  • Later, the Motion was smoothed and the End-effector was restircted to move in a straight line
  • The right side of the video is MoveIt visualized on RViz
