Harmonic Drive Gearbox

Incredibly Accurate and High Torque Robotic Gearbox

  • Harmonic Drive and Planetary Gearbox
  • Highly Compact
  • Dual On-Axis Encoders

  • Accurate to 0.05 Degrees
  • Low Backlash & Backdriveable
  • Highly Descriptive Project Documentation

Mechanical & Hardware Design

  • The real genius is the use of the space inside the FlexSpline
  • A Planetary reducer was places in the center of the wave generator
  • Using the Ring Gear as the output and the Sun Gear as the input, we gain access to the space inside the FlexSpline
  • In this space an on-axis encoder is placed to measure the output's movement
  • With the addition of the encoder on the input motor, this gearbox is highly accurate and compact

Materials

  • The Majority of the gearbox is 3D printed
  • The FlexSpline is 3D printed in Polycarbonate for the perfect mix of flexability and durability
  • The Circular Spline is 3D printed in Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polycarbonate for extra rigidity
  • An Aluminum machined ring surrounds the Circular Spline to prevent warping and tooth skipping under high loads
  • The remainder of the parts experience low forces and are 3D printed in PLA

Torque

  • At its peak, the 3D printed gearbox achieved 50 Nm of Torque
  • The video on the right is a test with 40 Nm
  • The limitation to the torque is actually the FlexSpline teeth skipping, not the input motor

Electronics

  • The input motor is controlled by a Moteus R4
  • The output encoder is an AS5048B
  • The "B" variant is specifically used for its I2C communication as it can easily be connected directly to the Moteus R4 and included in the CAN-FD pipeline
